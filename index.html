Cover Letter

The Veil Is the Brief.

Dear Madi and Leah - I've been studying your work long enough to know that what you build isn't content. It's clarity. And that's exactly what I do.

From the moment I landed on retreatmaia.com, I noticed something most brands get wrong: you've built a world that feels before it explains. Brown noise autoplaying. The word māyā unpacked before your services. Testimonials from real humans named Bridget and Chris. You are not selling wellness - you are creating conditions for presence. That precision is what drew me in.

I'm a recent Computer Science and Visual Arts, and my work lives exactly at the intersection where yours is heading. I've built AI-powered data platforms, designed UI/UX flows in Figma, run NLP models on human language, and spent four years making visual art. I don't treat design and analytics as separate departments. For me, they're the same conversation - one is listening, the other is speaking.

What I love most about this internship is the words "mold this role around a candidate's strengths." That's rare. And it tells me something important about how you operate. So here's what I'd bring to the table:

📊
Intelligence-Driven Social

I don't just post - I decode engagement patterns. Equinox-timed retreat drops, moon circle nights, Bali collaborations - your content has natural data architecture waiting to be mapped. I'd build that into every caption and story sequence.

✦
AI-Enhanced Design

I built Vogue Futures, an AI fashion trend intelligence platform - same principle, different vertical. I know how to integrate AI workflows into creative production without making the output feel synthetic.

🌿
Your Aesthetic, Amplified

Forest greens, linen tones, sacred geometry, clean serif type - I built this entire mood board to mirror your visual world. I'd be designing for Retreat Maia, not importing my own aesthetic onto yours.

🔗
UX Thinking in Social

From my UI/UX background: every touchpoint is a user flow. Your Instagram grid isn't a gallery, it's an onboarding sequence. I'd treat it that way, with intention behind every scroll stop.

A Pitch I'd Bring on Day One

"The Equinox Series" , A Seasonal Content System

You already time your retreats to equinoxes. That's a gift. I'd build a recurring content framework around this: each season gets a visual identity shift (colors, filters, typography weight), an engagement challenge rooted in that season's theme, and a data dashboard tracking which seasonal content drives the highest retreat inquiry rates. Ireland 2026 lands right on the Autumn Equinox — we start building the content arc 8 weeks out, shifting from "letting go" micro-content to full retreat storytelling. By the time registration opens, your audience has been emotionally prepared to say yes.

Data-backed. Soulful. Retreatmaia.com in content form.

I am genuinely moved by what you two have built, the way it started in a yoga teacher training and became retreats in Ireland and Costa Rica, a merchandise line, a corporate wellness arm, an archive. You're growing fast and you're growing with intention. I want to be part of that.

I'm available to start Summer 2026 and would love to bring both my analytical mind and my artist's eye to Retreat Maia's next chapter. Thank you for reading this far, and for creating work that makes people want to write letters like this.

With presence and precision,
